The takeaway

CL is a brand-new organic acquisition surface, shipped and scaling.

In 31 days, Character List went from zero search visibility to 6,012 Google clicks, 24,834 GA4 sessions, and ~40,000 unique users — while the rest of the site's organic traffic was −13% MoM under AI Overviews pressure.

CL's CTR is 2.4× the site average and its average Google position (4.0) is a full point ahead of the site baseline. The top query ("shield of sparrows characters") has 9.3% CTR with no AI Overview — a sign that list-format content may be structurally more resilient than narrative essays in the AIO era.

Honest caveats

  • • Selection bias. CL pages were built on our most popular recent titles (Shield of Sparrows, Theo of Golden, The Correspondent). Some outperformance reflects those titles' inherent momentum, not CL per se.
  • • Seasonality. Late March → mid April is peak student research season. A portion of growth is calendar-driven.
  • • GA4 tracking gap. Site-wide GA4 was broken for ~1 month through early April. Apr 1+ sessions are clean; earlier totals undercount.
  • • Can't isolate a "CL halo" on sister pages. Summary/CA views on CL-books held up MoM vs site-wide −13%, but selection + seasonality explain most of that.
  • • Conversion signal is early. First measurable CL → Chargebee checkout rate: 0.44% (Apr 6 cohort, visitors) — matches site-wide visitor conversion. Not yet enough power for a paywall A/B test.
