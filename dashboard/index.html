Methodology

How We Measure

  • 1 Normalize against site-wide trends. Absolute numbers are misleading when the whole site is declining. We track share-of-site instead.
  • 2 Weekdays only (Mon–Fri). Weekend traffic patterns are structurally different and add noise to comparisons.
  • 3 Multiple data sources. GSC for search visibility, GA4 for sessions, Amplitude for product engagement and real conversion (Chargebee checkout).
  • 4 Year-over-year comparison windows. Same calendar periods to control for seasonality.

Seasonality Controlled

YoY

Same calendar periods compared (Feb 15–Mar 14). The summer trough is seasonal, but the YoY decline across identical months is not.

The timeline rules out CL as the cause. In the Feb 15 – Mar 14 YoY comparison, CA impressions are up 18% and position improved from 6.2 to 3.7, yet clicks fell 37% (a loss of ~15,600 clicks/month). CL didn't even appear in search until Mar 20 — after this comparison window ended. CL's entire search history is ~1,100 clicks across 17 days. The decline is AI Overviews suppressing CTR site-wide, not CL displacement.

What about the Mar 20 – Apr 5 window? CA clicks did decline after CL appeared: 960/day (Mar 1–19) → 910 (Mar 20–31) → 770 (Apr 1–5), while CL grew from 0 to 134 clicks/day. On the surface this looks like displacement. But the entire site was declining in that same window — when normalized, CA's share held within its normal range (2.58–2.92%) and combined CA+CL share grew to 3.29%. We'll re-assess April 17 with enough data to confirm this trend.

Recommendation

Hold the CL Paywall A/B Test

We need more data before we can run a properly powered experiment

1

Only 17 days of search data

CL pages were indexed Mar 20. We need a minimum of 4 weeks to establish reliable baseline traffic patterns and control for weekly variance.

2

CTR advantage needs confirmation

CL's 2.89% CTR vs CA's 1.72% is promising but could be novelty effect. Need sustained data to confirm this is structural (e.g., list format resists AI Overviews).

3

80% non-subscriber traffic at risk

CL's search CTR advantage may depend on content being freely accessible. Paywalling prematurely could kill the advantage before we understand it.

4

Conversion rate too low for quick results

At ~0.4% site-wide conversion and 147 CL clicks/day, we'd need tens of thousands of visitors per test arm to detect a meaningful difference. Rushing an underpowered test wastes time and gives false confidence.

5

Re-assess April 17

At 4 weeks post-indexing: Is the CTR advantage holding? Is traffic volume sufficient for statistical power? Design the test then.
